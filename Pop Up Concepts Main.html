Core Concept

Diminishing
Returns

The point at which the level of profits or benefits gained is less than the amount of money or energy invested.

A principle stating that if one factor of production is increased while other factors are held constant, the output per unit of the variable factor will eventually diminish.

It is not that adding more effort or resources produces a negative result immediately, but rather that the marginal gain begins to shrink. The first hour of studying might yield a letter grade improvement; the tenth consecutive hour might only yield frustration.

Eventually, continuing to increase the variable input leads to negative returns—where the overall output actually decreases. It is a fundamental law underlying why infinite growth within a closed system is impossible, extending far beyond economics into biology, engineering, and personal productivity.
