**Operative Ontology** is the ontology someone *actually runs on* in practice, as opposed to what they would say if asked about reality. It is not a widely standardized term, but it is used in applied philosophy and therapy contexts.

The concept draws a distinction between two layers: the **declared ontology** — *"I believe all people are equal, I believe the world is basically safe"* — and the **operative ontology**, the implicit structure revealed by how someone *actually behaves, reacts, and perceives*.
