**Habitus** is Bourdieu's answer to a fundamental question: how do people consistently act in ways that *reproduce their social position* without being explicitly told to, without following rules, and often without even being aware of it? The answer is that their body, their reflexes, their aesthetic tastes, their sense of what's possible — all of it gets shaped by the conditions they grew up in, and then that shaped self goes out into the world and acts.

The crucial feature is that habitus is *not* habit in the simple sense. It's more like a **generative grammar of behavior** — a system that produces an infinite number of specific actions all consistent with a certain underlying structure. A working class kid and an upper class kid don't just have different opinions about universities — they have different bodies, different ways of walking into a room, different intuitions about what they deserve, different senses of *what is even for them*. That's habitus.

The **transposable** part is key: the dispositions formed in one field carry over into others. The way you learned to navigate authority in your family becomes the way you navigate authority at work. The relationship to risk you developed economically becomes your relationship to risk emotionally. You don't consciously transfer it — *it just moves with you*, because it's built into how you operate.
