**The Background** is the vast set of *pre-reflective* capacities, assumptions, and ways of taking things for granted that make all conscious thought and action possible — but which never themselves appear as objects of thought. Searle argued that every conscious act — every belief, intention, perception — *floats on top of* a huge iceberg of Background that is never itself made conscious. When you reach for a glass of water, you're not consciously thinking "glasses are graspable, water is drinkable, the floor will hold me, gravity is consistent." You just act. All of that is **Background**.

The crucial feature is that the Background *doesn't function like a belief*. You can doubt a belief, examine it, revise it. You **cannot** doubt your Background in the same way — because you need it *operating* in order to do any doubting at all. It's the ground you stand on to think, so you can't step outside it to inspect it — *at least not easily or quickly*.
