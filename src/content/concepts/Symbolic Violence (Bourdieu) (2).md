**Symbolic Violence** is the imposition of categories of perception and thought onto dominated groups in a way that is experienced *not as domination* but as natural, legitimate, or even chosen — with the complicity, often unconscious, of those being dominated. This is one of Bourdieu's most powerful and most misunderstood concepts. It's not metaphorical violence, and it's not about symbols in a vague sense. It's about a very specific mechanism: the way **power reproduces itself** by making its own categories appear as neutral reality rather than as interested impositions.

The key move is one of efficiency. Ordinary domination requires constant coercion — you have to keep forcing people. Symbolic violence is *more efficient* because the dominated **internalize the categories of the dominant** and then apply them to themselves. A working class person who thinks they're just "not smart enough" for university, a woman who thinks she's "naturally" less suited for leadership, a poor person who thinks their poverty reflects their personal failure — these are all cases of symbolic violence. The domination is real, but *it's being enforced from the inside*. This is what makes it more total than physical violence, which only controls the body: symbolic violence **controls the imagination**. It forecloses possibilities without physical force, shaping what people think they deserve, what they think they're capable of, what they think is *even for them*.

The **complicity** is crucial — and Bourdieu was careful to distinguish this from false consciousness in the Marxist sense. It's not that people are being deceived and would see clearly if only the ideology were lifted. The complicity is *deeper* than that. People apply these categories because they were formed by conditions that made those categories seem accurate. The working class kid who doesn't feel university is for them isn't being irrational — in their experience, it probably *hasn't* been for people like them. The categories fit the conditions that produced them. That's what makes symbolic violence so **self-perpetuating**.
