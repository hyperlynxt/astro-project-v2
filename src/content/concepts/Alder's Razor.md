Alder's Razor, or Newton's Flaming Laser Sword, is intended to be a sharper, more aggressive version of Occam's Razor. It argues that purely theoretical or metaphysical debates that have no observable, experimental consequences are ultimately unproductive.

If a proposition does not have a testable impact on the measurable world, it falls outside the realm of useful intellectual discourse according to this principle.
