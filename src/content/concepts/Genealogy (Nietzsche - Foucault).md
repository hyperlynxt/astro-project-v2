A method of historical investigation that asks not "what is the true origin and essence of X" but "how did X come to appear as natural, necessary, and true — through what accidents, power struggles, suppressions, and historical contingencies did this thing that presents itself as eternal and given actually get constructed?"

Genealogy is a direct attack on what both Nietzsche and Foucault saw as philosophy's deepest error — the assumption that things have **essences**, that current forms reflect some underlying truth, that what exists now exists because it is natural or rational or necessary. Genealogy says: look closer. What you think is natural is *historical*. What you think is rational is the residue of forgotten power struggles. What you think is necessary is *contingent* — it could have been otherwise, and understanding how it became this way is the only honest way to understand what it actually is.

Foucault extended genealogy from morality to entire systems of knowledge, institutions, and subjectivity. He asked: how did madness come to be understood as mental illness requiring clinical treatment? How did sexuality come to be understood as the deep truth of the self? How did the criminal come to be understood as a type of person with a pathological soul rather than simply someone who committed an illegal act?

In each case his answer followed the same structure. What appears as a neutral, rational, humanitarian advance — the asylum replacing the dungeon, the clinic replacing the scaffold, psychology replacing moral condemnation — is actually a transformation in the **technology of power**, not a liberation from it. The new form of power is *more total, more intimate, more efficient* precisely because it presents itself as care, knowledge, and truth rather than as domination.

His concept of **power-knowledge** is central here: Foucault argued that power and knowledge are not separate — power produces knowledge, and knowledge produces power. What counts as truth in a given era is not independent of who has the power to establish it as truth. The psychiatrist's ability to define madness is not just a cognitive achievement — it's a *power operation*. And that power operation shapes the reality of everyone it touches, including those who exercise it.

**Genealogy as a practice:** The genealogical method works by finding the ruptures, the accidents, the suppressions, the moments where things could have gone differently but didn't — and showing how the current form crystallized out of those contingencies while hiding its own tracks. It makes the necessary appear *contingent*. It makes the natural appear *constructed*. It makes the obvious appear as the outcome of forgotten struggles.

This is profoundly unsettling because it doesn't just change your beliefs about something — it changes the *ontological status* of the thing. Something that was part of the furniture of the world, something you stood on to think, gets revealed as a historical artifact. The ground shifts.
