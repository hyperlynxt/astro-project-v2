An Evolutionarily Stable Strategy (ESS) is a refinement of the Nash Equilibrium. While a Nash Equilibrium only requires that no individual can improve their payoff by switching strategies, an ESS requires that if a small number of "mutants" (individuals playing a different strategy) enter the population, their fitness must be lower than that of the residents.

Essentially, an ESS is a strategy that is self-reinforcing. Once it is established in a population, natural selection alone is sufficient to prevent alternative (mutant) strategies from successfully invading.

It is a fundamental concept in evolutionary game theory, explaining how complex social behaviors—like altruism or ritualized aggression— can persist over long periods of time.
