Hitchens's Razor is a fundamental principle of critical thinking and the burden of proof. It asserts that the responsibility for proving a claim lies with the person making the claim; if they provide no evidence, no counter-evidence is required to dismiss it.

This razor is a modern descendant of the Latin proverb: *Quod gratis asseritur, gratis negatur* (What is asserted gratuitously is denied gratuitously).
