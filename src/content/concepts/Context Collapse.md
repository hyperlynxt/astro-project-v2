Context collapse occurs when an individual is forced to manage a single identity for multiple, often conflicting, audiences simultaneously. In the physical world, we vary our behavior based on our environment (e.g., behaving differently at a bar than at a law firm). Digital platforms, however, often flatten these boundaries.

When LinkedIn, Instagram, and Twitter contexts merge, the individual must find a "lowest common denominator" version of themselves that is acceptable to all audiences, or risk performative exhaustion.
