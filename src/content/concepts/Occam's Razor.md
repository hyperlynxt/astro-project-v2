Occam's Razor, also known as the Law of Parsimony, is a problem-solving principle that suggests that simplicity is a virtue. When presented with two competing theories that make exactly the same predictions, the simpler one is the better one.

It is not a literal "razor" that cuts through truth, but a heuristic—a rule of thumb—that helps scientists and philosophers avoid over-complicating their models of the world. "Entities must not be multiplied beyond necessity."
