Hanlon's Razor is a philosophical shorthand for giving people the benefit of the doubt. In complex systems—whether they be software, bureaucracies, or social circles—errors are far more common than conspiracies.

By defaulting to an assumption of incompetence or oversight rather than active malice, we can reduce interpersonal conflict and focus on solving the underlying systemic issues rather than seeking a villain to blame.
