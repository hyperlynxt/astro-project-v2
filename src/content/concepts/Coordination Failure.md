Coordination failure occurs when a group of agents fails to achieve a mutually beneficial outcome because they cannot communicate, lacks trust, or cannot align their expectations.

Formally, in game theory, coordination failure is defined as a **Pareto-dominated Nash equilibrium**. This means that there exists another strategy combination that would make everyone better off (or at least no one worse off), but the current state is still an equilibrium because no single individual has the incentive to deviate on their own.

### The Intuitive Problem
Mutual gains are attainable by a collective switch to a different feasible combination of decisions, but these gains are not realized because no individual agent perceives a feasible way to increase their own gain by a unilateral deviation from their current decision.
