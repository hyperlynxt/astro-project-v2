Game Theory provides a mathematical language for describing and analyzing situations where the outcome for a participant depends not only on their own actions but also on the actions of others.

It is used to model everything from biological evolution to international diplomacy and corporate competition. At its core, it seeks to identify "equilibria"—points where no player has an incentive to change their strategy, given the strategies of others.

### Essential Concepts

- **Nash Equilibrium**: A state where no player can improve their outcome by unilaterally changing their strategy.
- **Pareto Optimality**: An allocation of resources where it's impossible to make one person better off without making someone else worse off.
- **Zero-Sum Games**: Situations where one person's gain is exactly equal to another person's loss.
