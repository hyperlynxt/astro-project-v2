word: Give someone an off-ramp
definition: "A phrase describing the act of providing a metaphorical exit strategy for someone, allowing them to withdraw from a position or commitment without losing face."
note: "The 'off-ramp' is a masterpiece of dialectical grace. It is a way of saying: 'you are wrong, but I am giving you a way to be right again.' It prioritizes the preservation of the person over the destruction of their argument."
