Ephemeral

adjective

Lasting for a very short time; fleeting or transitory.

"The glow of the tinkered construct was entirely ephemeral, fading as soon as the magic was spent."

"She recorded the ephemeral changing of the arcane currents in her field notes."

Etymology: Late 16th century: from Greek ephēmeros (from epi 'on' + hēmera 'day') + -al. Originally referring to fevers lasting one day.
Curator's Note

Perfect for describing artificer infusions. Don't get attached!

Related Words Evanescent Soon passing out of sight, memory, or existence. Transitory Not permanent; acting only briefly. Fugacious Tending to disappear; fleeting.
