/ˈsɔn.dər/ noun

Sonder

Coined Philosophy Emotion
The sudden realization that every stranger passing by is living a life as vivid and complex as your own — full of their own ambitions, friends, routines, worries and inherited craziness — an epic story that continues invisibly around you like an anthill sprawling deep underground, with elaborate passageways to thousands of other lives that you’ll never know existed, in which you might appear only once, as an extra sipping coffee in the background.
Etymology Source

John Koenig, The Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows (2012). From German ‘sonder’ (special) and French ‘sonder’ (to fathom).

Personal Note Hits different when you're alone in a crowded station. Everyone is the hero of their own film.
